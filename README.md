# Depths of the Forgotten

> *"Half a candle. Half a chance. Down you go."*

Forty floors. One torch. No way back up.

**Depths of the Forgotten** is a browser-based roguelike about descending into a place you were never meant to find. There's something at the bottom. It's been waiting a long time. Your torch will not last that long.

## ▶ [Play it in your browser](https://choppin32.github.io/Depths_of_the_Forgotten/)

---

## You've been here before. You just don't remember.

The dungeon doesn't care who you were on the surface. It strips you down to one number: how many floors deep you got before something with too many limbs got you first.

You'll fight things that whisper your name backwards. You'll find shrines built by hands that don't exist anymore. You'll loot a torch made from an infant's wick and feel it grow *lighter* every floor — not because it's burning down, but because it's feeding on something.

You'll die. You have five lives. They will not be enough.

---

## What you're walking into

🕯️ **Your torch is a resource.** Vision is light. Light is finite. The starter torch gutters out after three floors and the dark in here is *dark* — one tile in every direction unless you find a real flame. There are six torches hidden in the dungeon. Each one was made by someone desperate.

⚔️ **Six classes, six ways to die.** A Knight who tanks hits. A Rogue who sees in the dark. A Mage who melts faces and crumples on contact. A Berserker with no defense and no second thoughts. Pick your poison.

🎭 **Sixty creepy shrines, no repeats.** Each shrine in a run has its own name and its own awful little description. *The Cradle of Teeth. The Watching Dark. Altar of the Invited.* You will not see the same one twice.

💀 **Eight named bosses on a ladder of escalating dread.** The Rat King. The Bone Colossus. The Plague Witch. The Iron Golem. The Shadow Dragon. The Lich Lord. The Abyssal Hydra. And, at the bottom, the Forgotten King — who is exactly what he sounds like.

🎲 **Trinkets, relics, mutators, set bonuses, hidden items, locked chests, traps, elite enemies with random traits.** Every run plays differently. Most of them end badly.

🩸 **3-life permadeath.** Death isn't instant — but the dungeon is patient. It only has to win once.

## What it looks like

Pure ASCII. Monospace. Fog of war. The kind of game that lives or dies on how much your imagination is willing to fill in.

```
   ███████████████████
   █·······█···+·····█
   █···@···█·········█    @  you
   █·······█····C····█    +  shrine of something wrong
   █·······█·········█    C  chest (probably trapped)
   ███████████████████    >  stairs down
```

What you see is what your torch lets you see. Everything outside the radius is gone. Everything you've already explored fades to dim memory. Something in the dark remembers where you stepped.

> 🕯 **The Infant's Wick**
> *Every floor I survive, it grows a little smaller. I grow a little stronger.*

> 🕯 **Wick of the Martyr**
> *She burned for her faith. She burns still. Her light sees what the faithless try to hide.*

There are three more. They get worse.

## How to play

1. Open `index.html` in any modern browser. (Or [play it online](https://choppin32.github.io/Depths_of_the_Forgotten/).)
2. Pick a class.
3. **WASD** or arrow keys to move. **A**ttack, **D**efend, use **I**tem, **R**un, or trigger your class **E** ability in combat.
4. Press **?** in-game for the full legend and controls.
5. Find the stairs. Go down. Don't look back.

Saves automatically to your browser. Works on desktop and mobile. No account, no telemetry, no microtransactions, no patches that change everything overnight. It's just a game in a single HTML file. The way games used to be.

---

## Why one file?

Because that's how you make something that lasts. No build pipeline to rot. No dependencies to break. No framework to deprecate. In ten years you can still double-click `index.html` and the dungeon will still be there.

The dungeon will always be there.
